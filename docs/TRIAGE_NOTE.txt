TRIAGE NOTE — Operation Promise Keeper

This is the honest version of what I built, what I cut, and what I'd still lose sleep over.


1. Top 3 company-risk failure modes I found in the data

   a) Same goat promised to more than one customer.
      I found 7 of these in the festival sheet after resolving refs to canonical ids.
      Two families showing up on the 6th for one goat is the worst case scenario.

   b) Confirmed bookings on goats that won't be fit on delivery day.
      Withdrawal windows, dead status, quarantine — 7 bookings like this.
      Last year's near-miss with medicated goat is basically this category.

   c) Promises that don't resolve to a real animal at all.
      10 confirmed bookings where Vivek's animal_ref doesn't map cleanly to GoatOS.
      Ops thinks they're covered; the system can't point at a physical goat.


2. Which risk I attacked first, and why

   Identity resolution first. Everything else is garbage if you're looking at the wrong goat
   or silently merging two animals. I used union-find on GoatOS + legacy CPT tags, with
   explicit confidence levels. No merge on farm_number alone — that was a trap in the data.

   After that: delivery-day eligibility (June 6, not "today"), then reconciliation so
   the bad promises are visible with customer names, then safe booking with a lock so
   new double-assignments can't happen through the API.

   Pricing and inventory listing came in the same pass because you can't show a goat
   as "available" without a defensible price and weight.

   Later passes (same slice, lower panic order): replacement offers for fixable bookings,
   then feed contamination trace for promised goats in the bad shed window.


3. The invariant my code actually enforces

   One canonical goat → at most one active festival promise.

   What that means in code:
   - listAvailableAnimals() excludes any goat already on a confirmed row in festival_bookings.csv
     (after ref resolution) OR already booked in the in-memory runtime store OR assigned as
     a substitute via replacement store.
   - bookAnimal() takes a per-goat async lock, re-checks assignment + eligibility inside
     the lock, then commits. Two concurrent POSTs for the same id: exactly one wins (vitest
     covers this).
   - applyReplacement() uses the same per-goat lock on the substitute id so two ops can't
     grab the same replacement goat at once.

   Separate from that: reconcileBookings() surfaces the 7 double-books already sitting in
   Vivek's sheet — I didn't auto-fix those on load, but the UI can reassign the later
   booking to a substitute goat when one exists.


4. What I built beyond the first cut

   - traceFeedExposure() — cross feed_shed_log contaminated batches with animal_movements
     to see who was physically in CBE-S04 on the bad delivery days. 20 goats exposed;
     6 confirmed bookings flagged critical (not cleared before delivery). Shown in
     discrepancies tab + detail panel.

   - findSubstituteForBooking() + applyReplacement() — breed + promised weight match,
     delivery-eligible, high identity, not already assigned. Offers for ineligible bookings,
     field deaths, double-bookings (secondary customer), and feed exposure. 15 offers in
     the dataset; 3 of those are feed-related.

   - getReplacementOffers() split from reconcileBookings() so /api/discrepancies stays fast;
     UI loads offers in parallel.

   - Feed-exposed goats not cleared before delivery blocked from inventory/booking
     (`feedExposureSaleBlockReason` in `saleEligibilityBlockReason`), not only flagged on
     existing promises.

   - Promised-weight shortfall (weightShortfall.ts) — booked kg vs trusted kg today for
     resolved confirmed bookings. Gaps under 2 kg ignored; 2–5 kg medium; over 5 kg high.
     Example: BK-9364 (Haris Malik) booked 52.5 kg, trusted 43 kg → 9.5 kg short, still
     delivery-eligible but flagged. Also on Bookings tab as trusted/gap column.

   - Demo UI: inventory (breed/price filters, pagination), bookings list, discrepancies
     (severity filter, pagination), goat detail (eligibility, price, history, evidence),
     booking form, replacement buttons.


5. What I intentionally skipped

   - ingestFieldReports() — general multilingual WhatsApp pipeline. I did regex death signals
     for booked goats where GoatOS still says active (caught 2). Not full NLP.

   - Growth projection for weight shortfalls ("might still make promised kg by June 6").
     Current check is trusted weight as of simulation now only.

   - Price mismatch check (quoted price vs rate on booked_on date) in reconciliation.

   - Persistent booking / replacement store. Runtime state dies on server restart. Fine for
     demo, documented below.


6. What's still unsafe / needs a human

   - Legacy-only CPT goats (6 medium-confidence canonicals) — no health ledger rows keyed
     to them. I wouldn't sell those without ops sign-off.

   - Field report death matching is regex, not reviewed translations. Could miss Tamil/Kannada
     phrasing or false-positive.

   - Sheet double-books and ineligible bookings are flagged, not silently fixed. Someone still
     has to call customers unless ops clicks Replace.

   - Promised-weight shortfalls (4 at 2 kg+ threshold today) are flagged but not auto-fixed.
     Customer may still get a delivery-eligible goat that's lighter than the sheet promise.

   - 3 feed-exposure bookings have no substitute in pool (BK-6682, BK-9325, BK-9940) — hold
     or manual swap. BK-9325 also has a field-death discrepancy; still one physical problem.

   - Restart npm run dev → runtime bookings (BK-RUN-xxxx) and replacements disappear. Sheet
     bookings still block availability.


7. Next 24 hours (if this were production, not the take-home)

   1. Price audit on existing bookings vs booked_on rate
   2. Persist runtime bookings + reassignments (sqlite + unique index on canonical_animal_id)
   3. Weight shortfall growth projection to delivery date (not just today's trusted weight)
   4. ingestFieldReports() for non-death signals (movement, treatment mentions)

   Current numbers: 200 canonical goats, 119 safely available now (feed-exposed-uncleared
   unbooked goats excluded via sale gate), 40 reconcile discrepancies
   (16 critical / 19 high / 5 medium) plus 6 feed-exposure critical rows in UI (46 total
   when merged), 15 replacement offers ready, 58 bookings on sheet (+ runtime session adds).
